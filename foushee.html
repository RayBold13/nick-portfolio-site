Grammy-winning artist Fousheé released her highly anticipated third studio album, Pointy Heights, on September 13, 2024. This 10-track project marked a significant departure from her previous work, embracing a sound deeply rooted in her Jamaican heritage.
Headlines from day one
The album opens with "Birds, Bees," setting the tone with its infectious reggae groove and whimsical instrumentation. Tracks like "War" and "Feel Like Home" showcase Fousheé's ability to seamlessly blend Caribbean rhythms with her signature alt-R&B style. "100 Bux," co-produced with Steve Lacy, offers a fresh take on reggae classics, incorporating elements of "Pass the Dutchie" by Musical Youth.
Headlines from day one
Named after the land her grandfather purchased in Saint Catherine, Jamaica, Pointy Heights is a sonic exploration that blends reggae, ska, and alternative R&B, reflecting Fousheé's personal journey and connection to her roots.
